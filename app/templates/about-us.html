An Introduction to BLSJCL

Email: blsjcl@gmail.com

Faculty Adviser: Magistra Foley

Boston Latin School Junior Classical League (BLSJCL) is a chapter of the Massachusetts Junior Classical League (MassJCL) and the National Junior Classical League (NJCL), which is the largest Classical organization in the world. We are also the largest school chapter in the country, with over 300 members!

BLSJCL dedicates itself to the languages, culture, and literature of Greece and Rome. We hold events that encourage Latin and Greek students to gain an understanding of the ancient world, as well as share and develop a love for the Classics.

BLSJCL holds several events within the school year, including volunteer events and Coin Wars. Also, BLSJCL participates in MassJCL events, such as Kick-Off in October, Classics Day in December, Snowball in March, and State Convention in late April to early May, and even in Certamen tournaments!

BLSJCL meetings are in several places, such as the Dining Hall, sponsors' classrooms, and the Auditorium. In order to participate in these events, you must be a registered member, that is, having paid an $8 due to Ms. Foley in room 032. You also need to be a registered member in order to receive emails about JCL events.

Why have a club dedicated to the Classics? We study the Classics because much of our modern world is indebted to the cultures of the ancient Greeks and Romans. Because of this, we must understand the ancient world, so that we can better understand ours.

BLSJCL delegates have also won plenty of awards in the events throughout the year and at State Convention, including awards in Academics, Creative Arts, Graphic Arts, Olympika, and Certamen. At the 2015 State Convention, BLSJCL won first place overall, bringing home the Jane Rajotte Trophy for the second year in a row!

If you have any questions, please don't hesitate to contact one of our sponsors, or email blsjcl@gmail.com.

JCL Creed

We the members of the Junior Classical League, covenant to hand on the torch of classical civilization in the modern world.
We believe an acquaintance with the civilization of Greece and Rome will help us understand and appraise this world of today,
Which is indebted to the ancient civilization in its government and laws, literature, language and arts.
We affirm the JCL experience develops responsibility, fosters brotherhood, promotes enthusiasm, encourages competition,
inspires dedication and enriches our total growth.
